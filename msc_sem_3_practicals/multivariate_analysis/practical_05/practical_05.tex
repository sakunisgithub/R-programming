\section*{\faArrowAltCircleRight[regular] \textcolor{blue}{Question}}

\hspace{1cm} The following covariance matrix is estimated for a sample of 28 observations:

\begin{gather*}
S =
\begin{bmatrix}
128.72 & 61.4076 & -21.0211 \\
61.4076 & 56.9259 & -28.2963 \\
-21.0211 & -28.2963 & 63.5344
\end{bmatrix}.
\end{gather*}

The calculated mean values are $(8.8591, 4.50, 0.8571)$ and test whether these values agree with the assigned values $(5, 1, 0.2)$.

\dice{1} The test statistic for testing $H_0 : \utilde{\mu} = (5, 1, 0.2)'$ against $H_1 : \utilde{\mu} \neq (5, 1, 0.2)'$ is $$T^2 = n(\overline{\utilde{X}} - \utilde{\mu_0})' S^{-1} (\overline{\utilde{X}} - \utilde{\mu_0}).$$

Under $H_0$, $\dfrac{(n-p)T^2}{p(n-1)} \sim F_{p, n-p}$.

\begin{knitrout}
\definecolor{shadecolor}{rgb}{0.969, 0.969, 0.969}\color{fgcolor}\begin{kframe}

\hldef{n} \hlkwb{<-} \hlnum{28}\hldef{; p} \hlkwb{<-} \hlnum{3}
\hldef{x_bar} \hlkwb{<-} \hlkwd{matrix}\hldef{(}\hlkwd{c}\hldef{(}\hlnum{8.8591}\hldef{,} \hlnum{4.50}\hldef{,} \hlnum{0.8571}\hldef{),} \hlkwc{nrow} \hldef{=} \hlnum{3}\hldef{,} \hlkwc{ncol} \hldef{=} \hlnum{1}\hldef{,} \hlkwc{byrow} \hldef{=} \hlnum{TRUE}\hldef{)}
\hldef{mu_0} \hlkwb{<-} \hlkwd{matrix}\hldef{(}\hlkwd{c}\hldef{(}\hlnum{5}\hldef{,} \hlnum{1}\hldef{,} \hlnum{0.2}\hldef{),} \hlkwc{nrow} \hldef{=} \hlnum{3}\hldef{,} \hlkwc{ncol} \hldef{=} \hlnum{1}\hldef{,} \hlkwc{byrow} \hldef{=} \hlnum{TRUE}\hldef{)}

\section*{\faArrowAltCircleRight[regular] \textcolor{blue}{Conclusion}}

Observed value of the test statistic is less than $F_{0.05, 3, 25} = 2.9912409$. So in light of the sample, we fail to reject $H_0$ at $5\%$ level of significance.
